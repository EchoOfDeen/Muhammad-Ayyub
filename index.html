Shaykh Muhammad Ayyub

1952 – 2016

Biography

Full Name: Muhammad Ayyub ibn Muhammad Yusuf ibn Sulaiman Umar

Born: October 1952, Mecca, Saudi Arabia

Died: 16 April 2016, Medina, Saudi Arabia

Nationality: Saudi Arabian

Ethnicity: Burmese (Rohingya)

Occupation: Imam, Qari, Islamic Scholar

Alma Mater: Islamic University of Madinah

Resting Place: Al-Baqi Cemetery, Medina

Teachers: Khalil Al-Qari

Early Life and Education

Shaykh Muhammad Ayyub was born in Mecca in 1952 to a family of Burmese origin who had migrated to Saudi Arabia to escape persecution. He memorized the Qur'an under the guidance of Shaykh Khalil Al-Qari and pursued higher studies at the Islamic University of Madinah, earning degrees in Sharia, Tafsir, and Qur'anic sciences.

Career

He served as an Imam at various mosques, including Al-Masjid an-Nabawi in Medina. He was also a faculty member at the Islamic University of Madinah and contributed to the King Fahd Complex for the Printing of the Holy Quran.
